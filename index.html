About Me

I am an Electrical Engineer who loves hardware, software, and the systems that connect them.

A curious mind, a problem solver, and an aspiring graduate researcher.

Growing up with a fascination for how things work, I was naturally drawn to Electrical Engineering. What began as a curiosity about circuits and electronics quickly evolved into a passion for Control Systems and Industrial Automation — the engineering disciplines that make machines intelligent and responsive.

As a final-year student at FAST-NUCES Lahore, I have spent four years building a strong foundation across hardware design, embedded systems, signal processing, and software development. My final year project on Industrial Control Systems represents the peak of that journey — blending real-time hardware interfacing with intelligent automated control logic.

My internship at LESCO (Lahore Electric Supply Company) gave me firsthand exposure to real-world power distribution infrastructure, including 11kV networks and 132kV GIS grid operations. This experience deepened my appreciation for the intersection of theory and practice in electrical engineering.

Now, as I approach graduation, I am actively seeking a graduate-level opportunity to deepen my expertise in Control Engineering and Cyber-Physical Systems — fields I believe will define the future of smart industry and intelligent automation.
